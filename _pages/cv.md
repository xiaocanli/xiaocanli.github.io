# Xiaocan Li
Los Alamos National Laboratory, T-2, MS-B283, Los Alamos, NM 87545,
[xiaocanli@lanl.gov](mailto:xiaocanli.at.lanl.dot.gov), 5056677568

## Areas of Research
Particle energization processes in space, solar and astrophysical
plasmas: magnetic reconnection, collisionless shocks and plasma
turbulence.

## Education
* PhD in Space Science, The University of Alabama in Huntsville, 2011 - 2016
* MS in Space Physics, Peking University, 2008 - 2011
* BS in Surveying and Mapping Engineering, Tongji University, 2004 - 2008

## Research Experiences
* Feb. 17 – present, **Postdoctoral Research Associate**, Los Alamos National Laboratory
  - Studying particle acceleration during magnetic reconnection.
* Sep. 16 – Jan. 17, **Postdoctoral Research Assistant**, The University of Alabama in Huntsville
  - Using PIC simulations to study the interaction of plasma with electric tether.
* May. 14 – Aug. 16, **Visiting Student**, Los Alamos National Laboratory
  - Studying particle energization during magnetic reconnection using
  particle-in-cell and MHD simulations. I have developed a set of tools
  for post-analyzing the VPIC simulation results. These tools are used to
  study the role of particle drift motions, fluid compression and pressure
  anisotropy in particle energization. I have contributed to implement a
  new particle tracking procedure in VPIC using HDF5 and H5Part. I have
  been doing data analysis for several massive particle-in-cell
  simulations with several trillion particles.
* May. 12 – Aug. 16, **Graduate Research Assistant**, The University of Alabama in Huntsville
  - My thesis is on numerical simulations of particle acceleration during
  solar flares. I have developed a test-particle code to track charged
  particles in chaotic and turbulent magnetic fields using C, MPI and CUDA
  C. I have implemented a 2.5D particle-in-cell code to study particle
  acceleration during magnetic reconnection.
* Jun. 13 – Aug. 13, **Graduate Research Assistant**, Los Alamos National Laboratory
  - Studied particle acceleration and magnetic field amplification in
  supernova remnant shocks. I improved a stochastic integration code to
  solve Parker’s transport equation. The code is used to study particle
  acceleration at the shock downstream, in which the shock is generated
  using MHD simulations of a supernova remnant.
* Sept. 09 – Jun. 11, **Graduate Research Assistant**, Peking University
  - Monte Carlo simulation of the electron transport using Geant4.
