## Research Experiences
Find more about my research at [**[Research]**](/portfolio).
* Feb. 17 – present, **Postdoctoral Research Associate**, Los Alamos National Laboratory
  - I have been studying particle acceleration during magnetic reconnection in solar
  flares. Specifically, I study the major particle acceleration mechanisms using
  kinetic particle-in-cell simulations, develop particle acceleration models,
  solve the models coupled with MHD simulations, and compare the results with
  solar flare observations.
* Sep. 16 – Jan. 17, **Postdoctoral Research Assistant**, The University of Alabama in Huntsville
  - I studied the interaction of plasma with electric tether using kinetic
  particle-in-cell simulations 
* May. 14 – Aug. 16, **Visiting Student**, Los Alamos National Laboratory
  - I studied particle energization during magnetic reconnection using the particle-in-cell
  code VPIC. Specifically, I studied the roles of particle drift motions, flow compression
  and shear, and pressure anisotropy in particle energization.
* May. 12 – Aug. 16, **Graduate Research Assistant**, The University of Alabama in Huntsville
  - My dissertation is on numerical simulations of particle acceleration during
  solar flares. I developed a test-particle code to track charged particles
  in chaotic and turbulent magnetic fields, and I used the kinetic particle-in-cell
code VPIC to study particle acceleration during magnetic reconnection.
* Jun. 13 – Aug. 13, **Graduate Research Assistant**, Los Alamos National Laboratory
  - I studied the magnetic field amplification in supernova remnant shocks using
  MHD simulations, and particle acceleration in the shock downstream by solving
  the Parker transport equation coupled with the MHD fields.
* Sept. 09 – Jun. 11, **Graduate Research Assistant**, Peking University
  - I studied the electron transport using the Monte Carlo code Geant4.
