![alt text](/images/compress_pic.png)

Compression and shear acceleration in 2D magnetic reconnection. Left: electron
density and three components of the $E\times B$ drift velocity. The boxes with
solid outlines indicate representative regions with fluid compression, and
boxes with dashed outlines indicate regions with velocity shear. The top left
panel shows that the electron density increases from 1 to over 3, indicating
that plasma is compressed in the reconnection layer. The velocity plots show
that the flow can be compressed and sheared in the reconnection outflow region.
Right: time evolution of electron energization terms for the run without a
guide field (top panel) and the run with a guide field $0.5B_0$ (bottom panel).
The compression energization (red line) dominates the acceleration of
high-energy particles in reconnection with a weak guide field (top right
panel), and the compression and shear effects (blue solid line) are comparable
when the guide field is $50\%$ of the reconnecting component (bottom right panel).
This study connects particle energization by particle guiding-center
drift motions with that due to background fluid motions, as in the energetic
particle transport theory. It provides foundations for building particle
transport models for large-scale reconnection acceleration such as those in
solar flares.
See more details at [Li et al. 2018a](/publication/2018-03-09-paper1).

![alt text](/images/compress_parker.png)

We then study large-scale compression particle acceleration in magnetic
reconnection by solving the Parker (diffusion-advection) transport equation
using velocity and magnetic fields from 2D magnetohydrodynamics (MHD)
simulations of a low-beta high-Lundquiest-number reconnection layer. (a)
Electron energy distribution for cases with constant diffusion coefficients.
Lines with different colors indicate simulations with different guide field.
The dashed lines indicate power-law fittings. It shows that the
compressible reconnection layer can give significant particle acceleration,
leading to the formation of power-law particle energy distributions. We analyze
the acceleration rate and find that the acceleration in the reconnection layer
is a mixture of first- and second-order Fermi processes. When including a guide
field, we find that the spectrum becomes steeper and both the power-law cutoff
energy and maximum particle energy decrease as plasma becomes less
compressible. (b) Spatial distributions of high-energy particles for the
MHD run without a guide field at three different time frames. We choose
electrons with energies between 9 and 36 times of their initial energy. The
black lines are the magnetic field lines. This model produces a 2D particle
distribution that one can use to generate a radiation map and directly compare
with solar flare observations. This provides a framework to explain particle
acceleration at large-scale astrophysical reconnection sites, such as solar
flares.
See more details at [Li et al. 2018b](/publication/2018-10-05-paper2).
