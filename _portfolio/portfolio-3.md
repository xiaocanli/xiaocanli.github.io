Here we developed a macroscopic energetic-particle model for studying particle
acceleration at large scales. The model's foundation is built upon results from
kinetic simulations that the primary acceleration mechanism during reconnection
is flow compression and shear. As the first attempt, we solved Parker’s transport
equation, where compression is the acceleration mechanism, in a 2D reconnection
layer. We found that that the electrons are efficiently accelerated in the
compressible reconnection layer and develop power-law energy distributions.
See more details at [Li et al. 2018b](/publication/2018-paper3).
